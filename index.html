The Wonders of the Solar System



The Sun



The sun is the star of our Solar System and it is so big that 1 million Earths could fit in it. It is a ball of mostly Hydrogen and Helium, with other gases being present too.



Mercury



Mercury is the closest planet to the sun, and it is the smallest of all the planets. It is the first of the rocky planets. Mercury has a surface and atmosphere similar to our moon, and due to its extremely thin atmosphere, surface temperatures change drastically, ranging from -173 to 427 degrees C.



Venus



Venus is the 2nd planet from the sun, and it is the hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere that traps a lot of heat. Venus is the 2nd brightest object in the Earth's night sky, after the moon.



Earth



Earth is the 3rd planet from the sun, and it is the only planet that supports life. All of us live on planet Earth as it is the planet with the perfect conditions for us to live. 70% of the Earth's surface is water while the rest is land.



Mars



Mars is the 4th planet from the sun, and it is nicknamed the "Red Planet" due reddish color of its surface. Humans are trying to figure out ways to live on Mars so that if Earth dies, we can evacuate to Mars. We have even placed a rover on Mars to learn more about the planet's surface.



Jupiter



Jupiter is the 5th planet from the sun, and it is the biggest planet in the solar system, being 11 times bigger than Earth. It is the first of the gas planets. It is also the fastest spinning planet with 1 day being just 10 hours. Jupiter has a storm the size of Earth on its surface, and it is called the Great Red Spot. The Great Red Spot was discovered 200 years ago, and it still exists on Jupiter.



Saturn



Saturn is the 6th planet from the sun, and it is known for its unique rings. All the gas planets have rings, but Saturn's rings are the most visible. It has the most moons with an unbelievable 145 moons, and the biggest of these moons is Titan.



Uranus



Uranus is the 7th planet from the sun, and it is tilted on its side, making it spin vertically. Due to its tilt, it receives 42 Earth years of continous sunlight and 42 years of continuous darkness. Uranus's interior is mainly made up of ice and rock, making it one of the ice giants.



Neptune



Neptune is the farthest planet from the sun, and it has the coldest average temperature out of all the planets. Neptune was the only planet discovered by mathematical prediction instead of observation. Neptune's interior is similar to Uranus, made up of ice and rock, making it the other ice giant.
